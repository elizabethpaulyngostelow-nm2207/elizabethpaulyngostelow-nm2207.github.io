Examining Singaporean youth's attitudes and social tolerance towards serious mental illness


Have you ever heard an insult towards someone because of their mental illness?

While Singapore has made strides in progress over the years regarding the availability of treatments surrounding mental illness as well as avenues of support for mentally ill individuals and their loved ones, a glaring problem still remains.

How might we visualise this? Let us take a look at the data from a research paper conducted in 2017 on Singaporean youths’ attitudes towards mental illness – Stigma among Singaporean youth: a cross-sectional study on adolescent attitudes towards serious mental illness and social tolerance in a multiethnic population.

Section A: Sociodemographic breakdown of sample

The paper conducted an online survey from which 940 responses were used for analysis. For background context, the survey respondents from the research were primarily youths with a wide variety of identities as seen from this socio-demographic breakdown by gender, nationality and ethnicity.

With youths representing the direction in which society is moving in, they encapsulate emerging societal values. So just what do youths think about mental illness? Let us delve into the survey responses and find out.

Website About

Singapore Association for Mental Health

 Established in 1968, SAMH offers a wide multitude of community mental health initiatives for mentally ill individuals to rejuvenate their lives as well as to encourage a general sense of mental wellbeing. SAMH also reaches out to the loved ones of individuals with mental illness to give holistic support.

Mindline

 Mindline is a platform that welcomes and encourages everyone to seek help for mental health, offering an extensive array of resources such as self-assessment tools, self-care exercises, an AI chat to share what you are feeling and other external resources tailored by categories like caregiving or financial support. Mindline aims to banish the stigma surrounding mental illness and mental health.

Health Hub: MindSG

 Health Hub MindSG provides a curated selection of resources on topics such as what mental well-being is as well as how to care for others and ourselves. Dispelling myths such as the weakness of reaching out for help, MindSG aims to clarify common misunderstandings and encourage readers to take charge of their own mental health.
